Cathedris is an ongoing creative outlet -- it's a worldbuilding project for the sake of creation. It's created online, piece by piece, article by article, on the platform known as World Anvil. These articles detail the worlds history, its gods, species, countries and more. Many of the articles were created as part of platform-wide challenges or competitions, during which the combination of my writing and art has won many awards and accolades.

This world is dark and foreboding, playing with themes of hope versus despair within an industrial world. The world of Cathedris was created with the guidance of colossal gods that helped form it -- but Cathedris as a worldbuilding project begins when those same gods all "died". However, being gods, their bodies were too powerful to properly die. What remained were the "God-husks", giant mindless beings cursed to wander the planet for eternity.

This event that created the God-husks took place many thousands of years ago, and was the origin point of modern day Cathedris. Civilizations grew and matured under the shadow of these dead gods, and as a result, much of society is based around the way they impact the world within which they wander.
